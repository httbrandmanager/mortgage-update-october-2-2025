Mortgage Rates Inch Up After Decline:

What It Means for Central Texas Buyers

October 2, 2025

&#160;

After several weeks of steady declines, mortgage rates inched up again as of October 2, 2025, according to Freddie Mac’s latest Primary Mortgage Market Survey. The 30-year fixed-rate mortgage rose to 6.54%, up from 6.30% the previous week, while the 15-year fixed-rate mortgage climbed from 5.49% to 5.55%. Though modest, this increase reflects ongoing adjustments in the broader financial markets as inflation data, Federal Reserve policy, and economic growth continue to shape borrowing costs. For homebuyers across Austin, Buda, Kyle, and the Greater Central Texas area, staying informed about these rate shifts is key to making confident decisions.


Despite the slight increase, housing demand remains strong. Purchase applications are up 18% compared to last year, while refinance applications have jumped an impressive 42%, showing that many homeowners are still finding opportunities to benefit from improved rates earlier in the season. This surge in activity underscores the resilience of the Austin housing market, where buyer confidence continues to hold firm even amid moderate rate fluctuations.


Mortgage rate movements are influenced by several factors, including Treasury yields, inflation trends, and the Federal Reserve’s ongoing assessment of economic conditions. The recent uptick suggests that while long-term rates are stabilizing, short-term volatility remains. For Central Texas homebuyers, this reinforces the importance of monitoring market updates closely and being ready to act quickly when favorable lending conditions return.
